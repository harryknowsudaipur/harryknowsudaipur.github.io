Chapter 01 · Eat

Where I actually eat, when nobody's watching.

Three of about thirty. The full list travels with me — ask on the food trail and I'll walk you through it.

Paliwal Kachori, Bapu Bazaar

Only place I go for breakfast. Get there before 9 AM or the pyaaz kachori is gone. Skip the fork — eat it with your hands like everyone else.

Under ₹50 · 8–11 AM

Sunrise chai at Ambrai Ghat

Not the fancy restaurant — the vendor sitting on the steps at 6 AM. The City Palace lights up across the water. Quietest five minutes in India.

Free · get there by 5:45 AM

Shakti Nagar Chowpatty (Indo-Chinese)

This is where locals actually eat dinner. Ask for the schezwan fried rice with extra chilli. Zero English on the menu, all of the flavour.

₹150–300 · 7pm–late

Chapter 02 · Wander

Corners of the old city the map app doesn't know about.

01

The blue lanes behind Jagdish Temple

Walk uphill instead of down. Ten minutes in you're in courtyards nobody photographs — painters, a stray goat or two, old women shelling peas.

02

Sunset from Karni Mata (not the ropeway)

The ropeway is fine. The trek up the back is better. Twenty minutes, a temple, and the whole lake laid out below with no jostling for photos.

03

Bagore-ki-Haveli museum at opening

Go at 10 AM sharp. You'll have the puppet room, the miniature paintings and the balcony over Gangaur Ghat entirely to yourself for about an hour.

04

Iswal village, on the Ranakpur road

A twenty-minute detour I add to fort trips when guests are up for it. A working potter, a well, a Bhil hamlet. No entry fee. No tickets. No brochure.

Chapter 03 · Skip

And the ones I'd quietly steer you away from.

✕ Skip this

The generic "sunset boat ride"

Do this instead

Walk to Ambrai Ghat bridge. Same lake, same palaces, no queue, no ₹800 ticket. Bring a chai.

✕ Skip this

Silver shops on the main lake road

Do this instead

Prices are marked up 40% and half the pieces are machine-stamped. Ask me for the two artisan lanes in Lal Ghat.

✕ Skip this

The "authentic Rajasthani thali" near City Palace

Do this instead

It's a tour-bus canteen. Real thali is at family-run places in the new city — I'll point you to one that doesn't crop up on any list.
