Who's showing you around

Not an agency. One local, who's done this for 1.5 years and counting.

I grew up in Udaipur's lanes, not in a training manual. I've spent the last year and a half guiding travellers through the city on foot, by boat and by road — the palaces, yes, but also the tea stall where the boatmen actually eat, and the rooftop with no sign that has the best view of Pichola at 6:45pm.

I plan every route myself, come along for the whole day, and stay reachable before and after the tour if you need anything — a restaurant booking, a taxi at midnight, a translator at the pharmacy.

Inside a handful of protected monuments (like the City Palace complex), Indian law requires a government-certified guide to lead the walkthrough. On those days, I bring in a certified guide I work with regularly — you still get me for everything else, all day.
